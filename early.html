Introduction

Early computers were large, complex machines designed to perform calculations. They laid the foundation for modern computing.

ENIAC

ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer) was developed in the 1940s. It was massive in size and used thousands of vacuum tubes.

Colossus

Colossus was one of the first programmable digital computers. It was used during World War II to break encrypted German messages.

Difference Engine

Designed by Charles Babbage, the Difference Engine was an early mechanical computer used to perform mathematical calculations automatically.
